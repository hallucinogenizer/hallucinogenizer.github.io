xiv. three to skip next time

If you wanted PKR 4 lakh back this year, here's where it was.

Not the coffees. Not the gifts. Not the donations or the dependents or the cats. Three lines in this file are, on honest reflection, the ones with the lowest joy-per-rupee return. None will surprise you.

Skip One · PKR 1.14 L
The PTA tax on the OnePlus 13.
The phone was PKR 2.39 L; the PTA tax was PKR 1.14 L on top — a 47.7% effective import duty on a flagship you already had a working alternative for. The tax doesn't make the phone better; it pays purely for the right to use it in Pakistan.

If your previous phone was still usable, the entire PKR 3.53 L expense was the year's most cuttable purely-personal indulgence. If you'll buy a flagship again, the move is locally-distributed-and-warranted, even at a slightly higher sticker, because the PTA component shrinks. The PKR 1.14 L this year bought no joy — only a SIM lock.
Skip Two · PKR 65,901
The Nov 14 Dolmen Mall clothing run — four stores, one afternoon.
Monark (PKR 18,401) + Charcoal (PKR 17,680) + Walkeaze (PKR 14,910) + KIJI (PKR 14,910). Four wardrobes worth of clothing, every brand a different aesthetic, all in one trip.

Decision research is consistent on this: shopping in batches like this produces worse satisfaction-per-rupee than single, considered purchases. You buy variety because the trip is a treat in itself, not because the wardrobe needs four jackets. Half this clothing typically sits unworn within 6 months.

The kinder rule next year: one store, one item, with a 48-hour wait. Total avoided: realistic PKR 30–40k of underused clothing.
Skip Three · PKR ~73,000
Bose Speakers + Dawlance Air Fryer + Clinix Cushions + Journey Cloud accidental purchase.
The Bose Speakers (PKR 32,556) and Dawlance Air Fryer XXL (PKR 39,999) are quality-of-life appliances. The honest test is the uses-per-month count — if the air fryer has been used fewer than 8 times since Nov 2025 (most kitchens average around 5), your cost-per-use is PKR 1,000+ and a PKR 500 Daraz model would have served the same purpose.

The Clinix wedge pillow + travel pillow (PKR 13,501) and the Journey Cloud "accidental purchase" (PKR 14,200, tagged exactly that way in your own ledger) round it out. The Journey Cloud entry is the most striking line in the file: a four-figure subscription you actively did not intend to buy.

Honest verdict: subscriptions and one-shot premium-brand appliances are where small fortunes vanish without joy. A quarterly subscription audit, plus a "would I buy the Daraz version?" question before any premium-brand appliance, would have netted PKR 50–60k this year.
